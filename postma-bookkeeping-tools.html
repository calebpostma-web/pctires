You sold something — here's what to record
Every sale needs an invoice and an income entry in your Google Sheet.
Did the client already pay, or are you billing them now?
1
Go to Invoice Wizard
Click 🧾 Invoice Wizard in the top navigation. This creates a professional PDF invoice to send to your client.
2
Enter client details
Type the client name (e.g. Postma Heating Ltd.), their address and email. The invoice number auto-generates — leave it unless you need a specific number.
3
Add your line items
Click + Add line item. Enter a description (e.g. Consulting — November 2025), quantity (usually 1), and rate (dollar amount). The total calculates automatically.
4
Check the HST toggle
For most business clients, leave HST ON. Ontario 13% HST is added automatically. You collect this on behalf of CRA and remit it quarterly. Only turn it OFF if the client is HST-exempt.
5
Download PDF and send to client
Click ⬇ Download PDF to save the invoice, then email it. Most clients pay by e-transfer.
6
Copy the row to your Google Sheet
Click → Copy for Google Sheets. Open your Google Sheet ↗, go to the Invoices tab, click the first empty row in column A, press Ctrl+V. Then go to the Income tab and paste again.
💡 HST reminder: The HST you charge (e.g. $433 on a $3,333 invoice) is not your money — you're holding it for CRA. Set it aside and pay it quarterly.
1
Create the invoice anyway
Even if they've already paid, create the invoice in the Invoice Wizard. It's your official paper trail — CRA requires documentation for every dollar of income.
2
Note the payment in the Notes field
At the bottom of the Invoice Wizard, in the Notes field write: Paid via e-transfer [date of payment]. This creates a record of when money was received.
3
Paste into Google Sheet and mark Paid
Copy to Sheets, paste into the Invoices tab. In the Status column, change Unpaid to Paid and enter the payment date. Paste into Income tab too.
💡 Why create an invoice if they already paid? CRA can audit any transaction. An invoice proves the income was legitimate business revenue, not a personal gift or loan. It also helps you track who owes you money.

You bought something — here's what to record
Business expenses reduce your taxable income and let you recover the HST you paid (ITCs).
How are you recording this?
1
Download your statement (PDF or CSV)
AMEX: americanexpress.com → Statements → Download → PDF or CSV.
BMO: bmo.com → Accounts → Download transactions → CSV.
Save to Desktop or Downloads.
2
Open Statement Importer and select your bank
Click 📥 Statement Importer above. Choose AMEX Credit Card or BMO Bank Account to match the statement you downloaded.
3
Upload the CSV and click Analyze with AI
Drag your CSV file into the upload box, or click to browse. Then click Analyze with AI →. The AI reads every transaction, assigns a category (Fuel, Supplies, Professional Fees, etc.), and extracts the HST automatically. This takes about 10–30 seconds.
4
Review the categories
Scan through the results. The AI is right about 90% of the time. For anything wrong, click the dropdown in the Category column and fix it. Click ✕ on any row that isn't a business expense (personal purchases, credit card payments, transfers between accounts).
5
Export to Google Sheets
Click Continue to export → then choose Copy to clipboard. Open your Google Sheet ↗, go to the Expenses tab, click cell A in the first empty row, and press Ctrl+V. Done.
💡 Do this monthly. The best habit is to import your AMEX and BMO statements on the 1st of every month. Takes about 5 minutes and keeps your books current.
1
Have your receipt ready
You need: the date, vendor name, total amount paid (including HST), and what it was for. If you have a physical receipt, check if it shows HST separately — if not, it's still included in the total.
2
Open your Google Sheet — Expenses tab
Go to your Google Sheet ↗ and click the Expenses tab. Find the first empty row.
3
Enter the date, vendor, and total amount
Fill in columns: Date, Vendor/Payee, Description, and Total Charged (the full amount including HST).
4
Pick the right category and let the sheet calculate HST
Select the expense category from the dropdown. The sheet will automatically extract the HST (Total ÷ 1.13 × 0.13) and calculate the net amount. You don't need to do the math.
💡 Keep your receipts. CRA requires receipts for any expense over $30. Take a photo with your phone right away — the Receipt Scanner tab can log it digitally.

You have a receipt — here's how to log it
The Receipt Scanner reads the merchant, amount, and date from a photo and suggests the right category.
1
Take a clear photo of the receipt
Lay the receipt flat on a surface. Make sure the total amount, store name, and date are all visible and in focus. Good lighting helps a lot — near a window works well.
2
Open the Receipt Scanner tab
Click 📷 Receipt Scanner above. On a phone, this will open your camera directly. On a computer, you can upload the photo from your files.
3
Upload the photo and click "Scan receipt with AI"
Drag the photo in or click to browse. Then click Scan receipt with AI →. The AI reads the vendor, total, HST (or calculates it), date, and suggests a category.
4
Review and correct if needed
Check that the vendor name, amount, and category all look right. Use the Category dropdown to change it if the AI guessed wrong. Common corrections: Costco might come up as Supplies when it's actually a Membership.
5
Copy row to Google Sheets
Click → Copy row for Google Sheets. Open your Google Sheet ↗, go to Expenses tab, click the first empty row in column A, press Ctrl+V.
💡 Do this the same day. It takes 30 seconds. Letting receipts pile up turns a 5-minute job into a 3-hour nightmare at tax time.
👥
You paid an employee — here's what to record
Every payroll payment needs to be recorded as a Wages expense and CRA remittances need to be calculated.
1
Record the payment in your Google Sheet
Open the Google Sheet ↗ → Expenses tab. Add a new row:
• Date: today's date
• Vendor/Payee: Employee's name (e.g. Avienda Postma)
• Description: Payroll — [month]
• Total: gross wages paid
• Category: Wages & Salaries
• HST: $0 (wages are not subject to HST)
2
Calculate CPP, EI, and income tax deductions
Go to the 🗂 CRA Tax Filing tab → 👥 Payroll (PD7A). Enter each employee's gross wages in the Employee Roster. The tool calculates CPP (5.95%), EI (1.66%), and estimated income tax automatically.
3
Remit deductions to CRA by the 15th
The total shown in the PD7A section is what you owe CRA. Pay via BMO online banking: add payee "Canada Revenue Agency – Payroll", use your Business Number. Due date: 15th of the following month (e.g. January wages → remit by February 15).
4
Issue T4 slips by end of February each year
At year-end (or by Feb 28), go to 🗂 CRA Tax Filing → 📋 T4 Slips. The T4 slips auto-fill from your Employee Roster. Print or email one to each employee — they need it to file their personal taxes.
💡 No HST on wages. Unlike most expenses, wages do not have HST. Do not extract HST from payroll amounts — the full gross wage is the expense, no HST component.

You need to file or pay tax — here's what to do
There are three types of tax filings for Postma Contracting Inc. Here's when and how to handle each one.
Which tax are you dealing with?
1
Know your due dates
Your HST quarters align with your April 1 fiscal year:
• Q1 Apr–Jun → due Jul 31
• Q2 Jul–Sep → due Oct 31
• Q3 Oct–Dec → due Jan 31
• Q4 Jan–Mar → due Apr 30
2
Import all statements for the quarter first
Before filling the HST form, make sure all your AMEX and BMO transactions for the quarter are imported in the Statement Importer. This gives you accurate expense HST (ITC) totals.
3
Open the HST Return form
Go to 🗂 CRA Tax Filing → HST Return (GST34). Click ⬇ Auto-Fill from Importer to pull your totals automatically, or enter them manually from your Google Sheet.
4
Copy the numbers into CRA My Business Account
Click 📋 Copy summary in the HST form. Then log in to CRA My Business Account ↗ → GST/HST → File a Return. Enter the line numbers exactly as shown.
5
Pay the amount owing via BMO
Log in to BMO online banking. Add payee "Receiver General for Canada" (or "CRA – GST/HST"). Your account number is your HST number: RT 0001. Pay the amount shown on Line 112.
💡 Always file on time even if you can't pay. Late filing penalty is 5% of what you owe + 1%/month. Late payment is just interest (~8%/yr). Filing late is always more expensive than paying late.

Pay the balance owing by June 30
BMO online banking → payee "CRA – Corporate Tax" → account number is your Business Number (15 digits ending in RC0001). Pay the estimated amount shown in the tool.

Calculate what you owe
Go to 🗂 CRA Tax Filing → Payroll (PD7A). Update the Employee Roster with each employee's gross wages for the pay period. The tool calculates employee CPP + EI + income tax, plus employer's share.
2
Pay via BMO by the 15th
Log in to BMO online banking. Add payee "Canada Revenue Agency – Payroll Deductions". Your account number is your Business Number + RP0001. Pay the total shown on the PD7A form.

Record the remittance in your Google Sheet
Add a row in the Expenses tab: Date = payment date, Payee = CRA Payroll Remittance, Category = Wages & Salaries, Amount = total remitted. No HST.

Tax-Saving Ideas for Postma Contracting Inc.

Legitimate tax strategies for an Ontario corporation in consulting + lawn care. These are ideas to explore — always confirm with CRA or a tax professional.

💼
Income Splitting via Salary
Pay reasonable salaries to family members (Andrea, Avienda, Akiya, Ezra) who perform real services. Their income is taxed at their lower personal rate. Postma Contracting Inc. deducts the salary. Ensure work is real and salary is reasonable.
🚗
Vehicle Expenses (Log Required)
Keep a mileage log for any vehicle used for business. Deduct the business-use percentage of gas, insurance, repairs, and CCA. For a vehicle used 70% for business, claim 70% of costs. CRA requires a logbook to support the claim.
🏠
Home Office Deduction
If you use a dedicated home office space for Postma Contracting Inc., claim a portion of rent/mortgage interest, utilities, and internet. Calculate by area (e.g., 10% of home = 10% of eligible expenses). Must be the principal place of business.
📱
Technology & Equipment
Laptops, tablets, phones, software used for business are deductible. Phones are typically 50–100% deductible if used mainly for business. Equipment over $1,500 goes on Schedule 8 CCA. The Accelerated Investment Incentive allows 1.5× first-year CCA on new eligible property.
📈
Defer Income / Accelerate Expenses
Near year-end (March 31): delay issuing invoices until April if possible (defers tax one year). Also prepay or incur deductible expenses before March 31 — prepaid insurance, supplies, conference fees, professional development. Every dollar of expense = ~12.2 cents of corporate tax saved.
🏋️
EPSP / Bonuses to Reduce Corp Income
The corporation can declare a bonus to you (Caleb) payable within 179 days of year-end, deducting it in the current corp year. You'd pay personal tax on it, but it reduces corp income. Useful if corp income is near $500K SBD limit or if your personal rate is lower.
🌱
Charitable Donations via Corp
Donations made by the corporation give a federal tax credit of ~38.33% (vs. only ~15% for personal donation in low-income years). Make charitable gifts through the company to maximize the benefit.
🏛
Scientific Research / SR&ED
If any work involves technological advancement or developing new systems (e.g., your bookkeeping app development), it may qualify for SR&ED credits — 35% federal ITC on qualifying R&D expenses. Worth reviewing with a SR&ED consultant for the app development costs.
